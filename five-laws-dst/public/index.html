The Five Laws
of DST

The admissibility rules of the Deterministic Simulation Thesis — the five constraints a description must survive before it earns standing as knowledge. Two are stipulated method. Two are evidence-contingent. One is both.

methodological axiom — stipulated, load-bearing
empirical — evidence-contingent, falsifiable
hybrid — empirical + methodological

  1. Constraint Precedes Appearance

    methodological axiom

    Every realized outcome emerges through admissible transitions inside a bounded rule structure; possibility is filtered before manifestation.

    Meaning

    Nothing just happens. Name the rule-space that allowed the event and what it excluded before describing the event itself.

  2. Information Persists Across Transformation

    empirical · scope-limited

    Surface form may collapse, disperse, or disappear without that establishing deep informational erasure; physics has so far found ways to protect informational consistency under extreme transformation.

    Meaning

    Loss of form is not proof of loss of information. It vanished is a claim that owes an accounting, not a default.

  3. Description Is Layered, Not Singular

    empirical

    Reality may admit multiple valid descriptions across different levels, with experiential surfaces emerging from deeper relational or informational structure.

    Meaning

    Every claim belongs to a layer, and layered does not mean false. Interface means access, not illusion.

  4. Uncertainty Defaults to Observer-Local

    empirical compatibility + methodological default

    Probabilistic experience can arise from the observer's embedded position or limited access without requiring total-system indeterminism.

    Meaning

    Charge uncertainty to your position and your access first; root-level randomness has to independently earn the explanation. I can't predict it never equals it is fundamentally random.

  5. Only Cross-Framework Invariants Deserve Promotion to Theory

    methodological axiom

    A claim earns standing only when it survives comparison across independent domains — independence measured by ancestry, with frameworks sharing a load-bearing axiom counted once.

    Meaning

    Trust only patterns that survive restatement in two genuinely independent frameworks. Single-framing results are artifacts.
